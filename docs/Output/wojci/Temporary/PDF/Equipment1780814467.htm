What you need to cross-country ski is suitable equipment:

skis - compared to downhill skis, cross country skis are much skinnier—about half the width, in fact. This is because they are designed to tackle small hills at the most, with much of the terrain being primarily flat with occasional slight inclines

boots - they are usually soft to provide more freedom of movement at both the push-off and forward-extension phases

poles - they run longer than downhill poles, this added length will help give you more propulsion as you're skating across flat surfaces.
